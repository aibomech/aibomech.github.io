Before you design a robot arm, ask yourself these important questions:

  • 1. What task will the robot do?
    Will it lift, move, weld, or assemble something? The task helps you decide the structure and capabilities.
  • 2. Where will the robot work?
    This is called the workspace ‚Äî the area the robot must reach and move in. You need to know the size, shape, and environment.
  • 3. What will the robot carry?
    The weight (payload) affects the material choice, joint design, and motor power. You want it strong, but not too heavy!

Robot arms bring huge benefits to industry and daily life. They help to:

  • Work non-stop, 24/7, with no breaks!

Robot arms are inspired by the human arm. Here's how they compare:

  • Base: Like the human torso

Both are designed to reach, grip, and move objects ‚Äî just in different ways.

The workspace is the area the robot arm can reach. There are two main ways to analyze it:

  • Graphical Method: Use CAD software to draw the robot and see its movement range visually.
  • Analytical Method: Use geometry and math formulas to calculate reach based on joint lengths and angles.
