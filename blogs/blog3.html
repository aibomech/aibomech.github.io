üìå What You Need to Know Before Designing a Robot Arm

Before starting the design process, consider the following three key points:

  • 1. Define the Task: Know exactly what the robot is supposed to do. This helps in selecting the right configuration and functionality.
  • 2. Identify the Workspace: Understand the environment where the robot will operate, including how far it needs to reach and from which directions.
  • 3. Choose Construction Based on Payload: Depending on how much weight the end effector needs to lift, you can choose appropriate materials, element shapes, and dimensions.

Robot manipulators offer many advantages:

  • Save time and increase manufacturing efficiency
  • Quick return on investment
  • Reduce human exposure to dangerous or boring tasks
  • Improve accuracy and reduce errors
  • Work continuously 24/7 without fatigue

ü¶æ Robot Arm vs. Human Arm

A robot manipulator has parts similar to a human arm:

  • Base: Like the human body
  • Shoulder, Elbow, Wrist: These provide mobility and flexibility
  • End Effector: Acts like the human hand to perform tasks

üó∫Ô∏è Understanding the Workspace

The workspace is the area the robot arm can reach. We use two main methods to study it:

  • Graphical Method: Use CAD software to draw the robot and visualize its reachable area.
  • Analytical Method: Use geometric shapes and algebra to derive mathematical models of the workspace.

To ensure the design is correct, compare the results from both methods. If they match, your analysis is likely accurate.
