A machine without motion is just a piece of metal. Motion brings machines to life and defines how they behave in the workspace. In other words, motion is what gives a machine its job.

Every object in space can move in six different ways. These are called degrees of freedom (DOF):

  • 3 Translational Movements: Forward-backward, left-right, up-down
  • 3 Rotational Movements: Rotation around the X, Y, and Z axes

Understanding these motions helps engineers design machines that move exactly as needed ‚Äî no more, no less.

üîÅ From Rotations to Real-World Mechanisms

One amazing thing about machine design is how we can convert rotational motion into translational motion. This principle powers many real-world tools ‚Äî like cutting machines, drills, and robotic arms.

By studying motion and combining different types, engineers have built mechanisms that perform complex tasks with simple, repeatable movements.

ü§ñ How Do We Automate a Machine?

To turn a simple mechanism into an automated machine, we follow these basic steps:

  • 1. Define the structure and constraints
  • 2. Specify the workspace (where the machine can move)
  • 3. Study the degrees of freedom
  • 4. Derive motion equations to use in control systems

These steps help us model and control the machine using software and algorithms.
