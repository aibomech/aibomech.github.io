About Me

I'm Basheer Al-Tawil. I'm a robotics engineer and currently a PhD student working on mobile robotics, computer vision, and artificial intelligence. I enjoy building robots that can understand their surroundings and move around safely and efficiently.

Robotics Engineering

I work mainly with mobile robots, focusing on tasks like mapping, navigation, and path planning using ROS (Robot Operating System).

Computer Vision

I develop systems that help robots "see" using cameras and sensors like LiDAR, along with machine learning for detection and tracking.

Artificial Intelligence

I use neural networks and other AI tools to help robots make decisions and respond to their environment.

Embedded Systems

I design and program small control systems and microcontrollers for robots and automation projects.

My main research is about improving how robots sense and navigate by combining data from different sensors. I aim to make these systems reliable and practical for real-world use, not just for lab experiments.

I also enjoy sharing what I learn. Through the AIBOMECH, I regularly write articles, tutorials, and share open-source projects. I hope these resources help students, researchers, and anyone interested in robotics to learn and try new things. My goal is to make robotics more approachable for everyone.

Multi-Head Attention-Based Framework with Residual Network for Human Action Recognition

Sensors, 2025

This paper proposes a deep learning framework combining ResNet-18, Bi-LSTM, and multi-head attention for accurate and efficient human action recognition. It achieves 96.60% accuracy on the UCF-101 dataset and was validated on a mobile robot for real-time deployment.

Mobile Robot Navigation with Enhanced 2D Mapping and Multi-Sensor Fusion

Sensors, 2025

This paper presents an enhanced SLAM framework for mobile robot navigation that integrates RGB-D cameras and 2D LiDAR sensors. A novel fusion strategy projects RGB-D point clouds into 2D and denoises them alongside LiDAR data. The enhanced Gmapping (EGM) algorithm adds adaptive resampling and degeneracy handling, improving robustness.

A Review of Visual SLAM for Robotics: Evolution, Properties, and Future Applications

Frontiers in Robotics and AI, 2024

This review explores the evolution and recent advancements of Visual SLAM (V-SLAM) for robotic systems. It provides selection criteria and comparative insights into major V-SLAM methods, emphasizing their integration with ROS middleware and evaluating them using key benchmark datasets.

Design and Analysis of a Four DoF Robotic Arm with Two Grippers Used in Agricultural Operations

International Journal of Applied Mathematics Electronics and Computers, 2023

This paper proposes a novel 4DoF robotic arm with dual grippers for multitasking in agricultural applications. It incorporates kinematic and dynamic modeling using the D-H method and Lagrangian mechanics, while integrating with ROS for system-level control. The design enhances flexibility and compatibility with IoT and other agricultural hardware, offering a new approach to automation in precision farming tasks.

A comprehensive robotics project featuring a custom-designed 5 Degree-of-Freedom robotic manipulator developed from concept to implementation. The project encompasses mechanical design using SolidWorks CAD software, conversion to URDF (Unified Robot Description Format), and full integration with the ROS (Robot Operating System) ecosystem.
